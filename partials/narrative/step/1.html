Step 1 - Attaching the Rudder and Tiller

While it is not paramount that this step comes first, it is generally best practice to install the rudder first so that if any circumstance occurs while rigging, and the boat becomes free from its mooring, you will have the most control possible.



On most all small boats the rudder is attached via down facing pins (pintiles) which are inserted into holes (gudgeons) on the back of the boat.

After you have installed and secured the rudder, ususally with a locking pin, you can install the tiller. The tiller is the long shaft with which you turn the rudder. It gets secured by insterting it into a socket on top of the rudder and is held in place with locking pins.
