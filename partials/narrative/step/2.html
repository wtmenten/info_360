Step 2 - Attaching the Jib

The next part of boat rigging is attaching your jib, the small sail at the front of the boat.

The first part of rigging the jib is attaching it to its halyard, the rope that pulls the jib up and keeps it up. It is normally secured to the mast when stored. The halyard has a shackle (right) on one end. This is what gets attached to the ring at the top of the jib.

After you have attached the jib, grab the other end of the halyard. It is time to clip the jib to the forestay.

While hold onto the end of the halyard, start at the top of the jib and clip it on to the forestay. Keep pulling the jib up as you go until finished.

Once the jib is fully raised, secure the halyard to the front of the mast with a cleat hitch and run the jib sheets (ropes) through their cams (holsters) on the outer front of the cockpit.
